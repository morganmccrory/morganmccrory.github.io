How The Internet Works - A Short Story


Photo: Know Your Meme



If you’re anything like me, you spend quite a lot of time on the internet – but do you actually know how it works? No, it’s not a series of tubes. There are no pipes involved. It’s actually just a set of instructions. Here is a short YouTube Video to give you a short introduction.



Every website that you visit (such as this one!) is stored on a physical server somewhere in the world. A server is a computer where content lives, so each website takes up a certain amount of space on that server. When you navigate to its address, the file then gets broken down into thousands of packets. Each packet is then routed separately through devices known as routers.These routers take each tiny packet and identify where it came from and where it is going. From there, it decides where it should go next. When a server receives your request, it finds the web page that you are looking for and then sends it back to you. The packets go from router to router until they reach your computer. When they finally reach your computer, they are reassembled. And then, boom! You see the content on your computer screen. Here is another great resource: There and Back Again: A Packet’s Tale.
